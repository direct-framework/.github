# Contributing

DIRECT depends on the continued involvement of the digital research community.
By working together, we can build a shared framework that recognises digital expertise, strengthens research communities, and supports the future of digital research.

DIRECT is an open source project and anyone is welcome to contribute suggestions, feedback and/or pull requests to address any open issues in [the relevant repository](https://github.com/orgs/direct-framework/repositories) within the DIRECT organisation on GitHub. 
You can also open a new issue if your idea is not yet mentioned anywhere else. 

## Contributor Agreement

By contributing, you agree that we may redistribute your work under [our license](LICENSE.md). 
In exchange, we will address your issues and/or assess your change proposal as promptly as we can, and help you become a member of our
community. 

All contributors agree to abide by our [Code of Conduct](./CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

## Who Should Contribute?

We welcome contributions from individuals, institutions, training providers, and funders, whether through framework development, use case testing, training alignment or governance support.

## How to Contribute

The easiest way to get started is to file an issue to tell us about an error. This is a good way to
introduce yourself and to meet some of our community members.

1. If you do not have a [GitHub][github] account, you can [send us comments by
   email][contact]. However, we will be able to respond more quickly if you use
   one of the other methods described below.

2. If you have a [GitHub][github] account, or are willing to [create
   one][github-join], but do not know how to use Git, you can report problems
   or suggest improvements by [creating an issue][issues]. This allows us to
   assign the item to someone and to respond to it in a threaded discussion.

3. If you are comfortable with Git, and would like to add or change content,
   you can submit a pull request (PR).
